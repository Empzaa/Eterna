#### #Species
### Physical Description 
Stratusmoths are enormous creatures, reaching nearly a kilometer across in some circumstances. They are floating creatures and are analogous to a giant living blimp, with an incredibly thin layer of membrane skin that surrounds the gas that makes up most of the body of a Stratusmoth

### Habitat
Stratusmoths are only found in the [[Tsutia Desert]], although they have been rarely spotted in the [[Boba Redar Plains]]

### Diet  
Stratusmoths are carnivores, eating a plankton adjacent species called [[Luminids]].

### Lifespan  
Stratusmoths never stop growing, which is what eventually ends them as they are unable to hold in the large amounts of gas. They typically live several hundred years, but can live past a thousand.

---

### Role in the Ecosystem

###  Function/Role  
Stratusmoths are the central species of the ecosystem of the [[Tsutia Desert]], with their movement generating the sandstorms that so many species rely on, along with generating electricity from the breakdown of the toxins within Luminids

---

### Interactions with Other Species

###  Prey 
Stratusmoths only hunt [[Luminids]], functioning similar to the relation between whales and plankton.

###  Predators
Only humans, as they are the only creatures that can think through methods of attack on the airborne titans.

###  Symbiotic Relationships
It has a mutualist relationship with a majority of creatures in the ecosystem, as they provide the environment and electricity needed to survive, while the creatures below generally deter humans.

###  Competitors
N/A, they are the only creature that eats Luminids in large numbers as they are the only species adapted to purge the large amounts of toxins.

---

### Behavior and Adaptations

### Social Structure
Stratusmoths travel in packs of incredibly varied sizes, going from just individuals to groups of twenty or more. They lack a higher intelligence and are rather simple creatures, with little in the way of social structure

###  Defensive Mechanisms
Stratusmoths passively defend themselves from the large storms they create, alongside the electricity they generate, which prevents a majority of creatures from approaching them.

###  Reproduction
Stratusmoths are unique among animals in their method of reproduction, acting far more like plants in their release of spore-like clouds that allow for mating.
